# **For Users**

## **How do I drop a Q that I can no longer cover?**

1. ### **Navigate to the event**

* Open the F3 Nation Calendar  
* Click **“Show only my events”**  
* Find your event  
* Refer to [Signing Up to Q](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1O8u21U9w2Hg3XnYr5ddaWrmJgu5UoAk4d5rj1g3A4os/edit?tab=t.nmu55z13mu13) for instructions on accessing the calendar

2. ### **Remove yourself from the Q slot**

* Tap the “**More”** button next to your event on mobile, or click the “**…”** button on web/desktop  
* Tap/click “**Edit Preblast”**  
* Tap/click “**Take myself off Q**” button at the bottom of the preblast form

## **How can I change my name in the calendar?**

1. ### **Open your user settings in the F3 Nation app**

* Either search for F3 Nation Settings in the search bar and tap/click “**Open F3 Nation Settings”** or  
* Type “**/f3-nation-settings**” into a channel and send the message  
* Tap/click “**User Settings**” 

2. ### **Set your name and home region**

* Edit your user name in the top text box  
* Note that your user name should **not** include any region designations, because…  
* In the text box below User Name, you can select your home region, if you would like  
* As you start typing the name of your region, you should see its name appear in the drop down  
* Tap/click your region name to set your home region  
* You can also upload a profile picture here. This will show up in PAX Vault

 

## **What is the proper way to document Downrange posters?** 

1. ### **If the PAX is a member of your slack**

* Regardless of home region, members in your slack should be tagged in the PAX section.  
* A user’s home region is set independently.    
* Tagging them as PAX vs. DR PAX in the backblast hinges on whether the PAX in question is a member of your Slack.

2. ### **If the PAX is not a member of your slack**

* Search for the PAX in the downrange section of the backblast.  
* If they are a member of a region that uses the Nation app, you will find them when searching and be able to add them.  
* You can also manually enter PAX names, separated by commas

3. ### **If you travel to a region that does not use the Nation App or PAXMiner**

* Post a backblast in your home Slack’s Downrange channel, if one exists.  
* Otherwise, ask one of your region’s admins how to handle this situation.

## **How are the maps and the app related?**

1. ### **The maps and the app share data regarding locations, AOs, and events.**

* i.e., changes made to the maps affect what you see in the calendar and vice versa.  
* Specifically, if you have an ongoing series of events scheduled, you should be able to find it on the map and edit it in the app.

2. ### **Which is the preferred way to make edits (Best Practice)?**

	

* Prior to installing the F3 Nation App, make sure all of your maps are up to date.  Use the F3 Nation Maps admin to make sure your map information is accurate, concise, and consistent (e.g., if an AO has multiple days, the location, description, etc match across days).   
  * The F3 Nation App will use the map data to try to populate your locations, events, and AOs upon install, so this will go a long way towards making sure your region’s calendar looks accurate right off the bat.  
  * **NOTE:** It is possible that not everything will be imported into the app correctly.  You will need to make the appropriate edits to your locations, AOs and events.  
* After installing the F3 Nation App, you should rely on “Calendar Settings” in the F3 Nation App for editing locations, AOs and events.  Changes made in the app will be reflected on the map when it next updates. 

## **When do we stop using QSignups after installing the F3 Nation App?**

* Prior to your migration date, your region will use both QSignups and F3 Nation Calendar for scheduling.  
* Use QSignups for dates prior to the migration date.  
* Use F3 Nation Calendar for dates beginning with your migration date.  
* **NOTE:** Information in QSignups will not be migrated.  Have PAX move their signups prior to the migration date.

## **How can an improper backblast be removed from the database?**

1. ### **Open the calendar settings**

* Either search for F3 Nation Settings in the search bar and tap/click “**Open F3 Nation Settings”** or  
* Type “**/f3-nation-settings**” into a channel and send the message  
* Tap/click “**Calendar Settings**” 

2. ### **Remove the event associated with the improper backblast**

* Tap the “**More**” or click the “**...**” next to  “**Manage Single Events**”  
* Tap/click “**Edit or Deactivate Single Event**”   
* Locate the event you want to remove and tap/click “**Edit or Delete**”  
* Select “**Delete**” and click “**Yes, I’m sure**” when warned that this action is permanent

## **Why is “Unknown AO” showing up in PAX Vault?  How Can I fix it?**

1. ### **Unknown AO shows up when you have backblasts migrated over from your region’s PAX Miner database that don’t have a match in the new Nation App.**

* E.g., you may have posted in the past at an AO that closed, so you didn’t create a channel for it in the Nation app.  
* E.g., you may have previously had a channel dedicated to something like Q Source but now that is an event type, you didn’t create a Q Source AO in the Nation App.


2. ### **The Fix**

	

* Once you figure out which AOs (i.e., with Paxminer/Slackblast any channel that you had slackblast enabled in was an “AO”) are missing, use the Nation Calendar Settings to create a new AO for each of them.   
  * Either search and tap/click “**Open F3 Nation Settings**” or use the “**/f3-nation-settings**” command  
  * Tap/click “**Calendar Settings**”  
  * Tap/click “**Manage AOs**” and then tap/click “**Add AO**” from the drop down  
  * Enter the name of the AO and the channel to associate with it  
  * **NOTE:** You do not have to worry about creating a location or scheduling events for these AOs.  
* Use “**Paxminer Mapping**” in the F3 Nation Settings to match the old backblasts to the AOs you just created.  
  * Open F3 Nation Settings as outlined above  
  * Tap/click “**Paxminer Mapping**”  
  *  Select the “**Originating Channel**” from the drop down or by typing in the name of the channel that backblasts would have been posted to under the old system  
  * You should see three more options now:  
    * **Assign to Region** should already have the name of your region selected  
    * **Assign to AO** should be set to the AO you just created in the Nation App  
    * **Assign to Event Type** should be set to the relevant type of event for the backblasts in question
